Why Conservation Matters

At our online zoo, we care deeply about protecting endangered animals and their habitats. Learn about some amazing creatures and how you can help save them!

Tiger

Why Tigers Are Endangered: Tigers are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, climate change, and human-wildlife conflict. These threats have greatly reduced their population in the wild.

We can save tigers by protecting their habitats, stopping illegal hunting, supporting conservation efforts, and raising public awareness.

Amur Leopards

Why Amur Leopards Are Endangered: The Amur leopard is endangered due to poaching and loss of its forest habitat in eastern Russia and China. Its population is very small, making it vulnerable to inbreeding.

We can save it by protecting its natural habitat and enforcing anti-poaching laws. Breeding programs in conservation centers also help increase its numbers.

Javan Rhinos

Why Javan Rhinos Are Endangered: Javan rhinos are critically endangered because of habitat loss and past hunting. Today, only a few survive in one national park in Indonesia.

Protecting this last habitat and monitoring the remaining rhinos closely is key. Preventing human encroachment and increasing security can support their survival.

Mountain Gorilla

Why Mountain Gorillas Are Endangered: Mountain gorillas are endangered due to habitat destruction, poaching, and disease. Human activities like farming and deforestation are major threats.

Conservation programs, ecotourism, and community education have helped increase their numbers. Continued support for protected areas is essential.

Sumatran Tiger

Why Sumatran Tigers Are Endangered: The Sumatran tiger is endangered from illegal hunting and shrinking forests. Palm oil plantations and logging have destroyed much of its habitat

We can help by supporting sustainable farming and protecting forest reserves. Stricter anti-poaching efforts are also needed.

Vaquitas

Why Vaquitas Are Endangered: The vaquita, the world's rarest marine mammal, is critically endangered due to getting caught in illegal fishing nets. There are fewer than 10 left.

Saving them requires banning gillnet fishing in their habitat and improving patrols. Public pressure on governments can help enforce these actions.

Hawksbill Turles

Why Hawksbill Turles Are Endangered: Hawksbill turtles are endangered due to illegal trade of their beautiful shells and destruction of coral reef habitats. Climate change also affects their nesting sites.

We can protect them by banning the turtle shell trade and preserving coral reefs. Beach conservation efforts also help protect their nests and hatchlings.

African Wild Dogs

Why African wild dogs Are Endangered: African wild dogs are endangered because of habitat loss, disease from domestic animals, and human conflict. Their packs are small and easily disrupted.

We can help by preserving large protected areas and vaccinating domestic dogs nearby. Educating local communities can reduce conflict and support conservation.

Oranguntan

Why Orangunatn Are Endangered: Orangutans are endangered because of deforestation, especially for palm oil plantations, and illegal wildlife trade. They are losing their forest homes at an alarming rate.

We can help by supporting sustainable palm oil, protecting rainforests, and rescuing and rehabilitating captured orangutans. Education and stronger laws are also crucial.
