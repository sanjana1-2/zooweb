Reptile Species

King Cobra

King cobras are impressively venomous, large snakes native to Asia. They are called king cobras because they can kill and eat cobras. A full-grown king cobra is yellow, green, brown or black, typically with yellow-white crossbars or chevrons.

Indian Python

Indian pythons are among the largest snakes in the world, averaging 3 meters (9 feet 10 inches) in length, but can grow up to 6 meters (20 feet). Their bodies are thick and covered in smooth, iridescent scales. They have a distinctive triangular head with heat-sensitive pits on their snouts.

Crocodile

Crocodiles are large, aquatic reptiles with powerful jaws and armored bodies. They inhabit rivers, lakes, and wetlands, and are known for their ambush hunting techniques.

Chameleon

Chameleons are small to medium-sized reptiles known for their color-changing abilities and long, sticky tongues. They are primarily arboreal and native to Africa and Madagascar.

Monitor Lizard

Monitor lizards are large reptiles with elongated heads, powerful limbs, and long tails. They are carnivorous and found in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Indian Star Tortoise

The Indian star tortoise is a small tortoise with a distinctive shell adorned with star-shaped patterns. These patterns help them camouflage in their natural habitats.
